Model Control Panel LLM, emotion scoring & mood tuning

Everything here is picked up live — no restart. The preference/opinion engine uses the DeepSeek (or Ollama) connection; the emotion & mood scoring below drives the agent's moods.

{# ---------------- LLM connection ---------------- #}

LLM connection

Backend for the preference engine & opinion playground.

{# ---------------- Evolution tuning ---------------- #}

Preference evolution

How the agent's likes & traits drift over time.

Learning speed — how fast likes move each time something is observed (higher = quicker to change its mind). SOUL anchor — how far a personality-seeded like is allowed to drift (keeps core traits from flipping). Trait drift — how far personality can wander from its baseline.

{# ---------------- Mood granularity ---------------- #}

Mood granularity

How finely and how far back mood is read.

The mood is read from small rounds of a few back-and-forth exchanges instead of a whole sitting. Exchanges per read — fewer = mood reacts faster within a chat, more = smoother/slower. How far back — how many recent rounds the mood weighs (the "Recent rounds" window).

{# ---------------- Emotion scoring ---------------- #}

Emotion scoring

What reads emotions & moods from each round.

The Local LLM reads each round's user & agent emotions and the agent's moods in one pass — an uncensored local model handles roleplay/adult content best. Switching backend applies on the worker's next poll.

{# ---------------- Emotion taxonomy (chip editor, full width) ---------------- #}

Emotion vocabulary

The set of emotions the scorer is allowed to use. Type one and press Enter to add; click × to remove.

Add domain words GoEmotions lacks (e.g. lust, horny, yearning, tenderness). Mood rules can target any word here.

{# ---------------- LLM mood votes ---------------- #}

Mood votes from the AI

Let the LLM's mood read vote alongside the rules.

Confidence — how sure the AI must be (0–10) for a round to count as a mood. Loudness — how many votes each qualifying round casts. Together these let the AI carry a mood the hand-written rules missed.

{# ---------------- Natural transitions ---------------- #}

Natural transitions

Keep mood changes smooth & sensible, not jumpy.

Stickiness — how strongly the current mood clings on. Higher = moods last longer and flip less. Jump resistance — how hard it is to leap to an unrelated mood (one not connected in the map below). Higher = the mood walks step-by-step to a neighbour instead of teleporting.

{# ---------------- Transition map (full width) ---------------- #}

Transition map

Which moods can follow which — the paths the mood is allowed to walk.

A JSON object mapping each mood to the moods it can move to (directed — escalation and cool-down differ). Example: {"flirty": ["horny", "content"]} means from Flirty the mood may heat up to Horny or settle to Content, but not jump straight to, say, Focused. Give every mood a path back to a hub like content so it can always recover. Moods left off a list can still be reached, but only when their signal is strong enough to beat the jump resistance above.

{# ---------------- Low-signal emotions (full width) ---------------- #}

Background emotions to ignore

Checked emotions are filtered out of the "top emotions" — they show up on nearly every message and drown out the distinctive ones. Driven by the saved vocabulary above.
